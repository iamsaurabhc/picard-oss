# Picard Commercial License (template)


## Summary

**Picard OSS** is offered under the [GNU Affero General Public License v3.0](LICENSE)
(AGPL-3.0) for open-source use, evaluation, and local development.

If your organization needs to use Picard **without AGPL obligations** — for example
production deployments, multi-user hosted services, OEM embedding, or proprietary
forks you do not wish to open-source — you may obtain a **Picard Commercial License**
from the licensor.

## Who typically needs a commercial license

Consider a commercial license if you:

- Operate **Picard as a network service** (SaaS, managed offering, or internal
  multi-tenant API) for users outside your AGPL compliance plan
- **Distribute** Picard or a derivative as part of a commercial product without
  releasing corresponding source under AGPL
- Need **legal certainty** that modifications, integrations, and deployment
  artifacts remain proprietary
- Require **enterprise terms**: support SLAs, indemnification, or named-entity licensing

## Who can usually stay on AGPL (no fee)

You may use Picard under AGPL-3.0 at no charge when you:

- Run Picard **locally** on your own machine for personal study, proof-of-concept,
  or non-production evaluation
- Use the **local-first** stack (`./scripts/start.sh`, data under `.picard-data/`)
  without offering a competing hosted product built on unreleased modifications
- **Comply with AGPL** for any distribution or network use (including providing
  source to users who interact with your modified version over a network)

## What a commercial license typically includes

Subject to a signed agreement and payment, a commercial license may grant:

| Grant | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| **Use** | Production deployment of the licensed Picard software version(s) |
| **Modify** | Internal modifications without obligation to publish source |
| **Distribute** | Embed in commercial products per agreement scope |
| **SaaS / network use** | Offer Picard-based services without AGPL source-distribution triggers |
| **Sublicense** | As explicitly defined in the agreement (often limited to affiliates) |

**Excluded unless stated:** trademarks (`Picard` name/logo), patents not expressly
granted, and versions outside the licensed release range.

## What remains open under AGPL

Without a commercial license, the public repository stays **AGPL-3.0**. That includes:

- Running unmodified releases for local development and evaluation
- Contributing improvements back under AGPL (see [Contributing](#contributing))
- Forking publicly, subject to AGPL copyleft on distribution and network use

## Licensing models (choose in your agreement)

Pick one or combine in the signed contract:

1. **Per deployment** — production instance or environment
2. **Per organization** — unlimited internal deployments, named legal entity
3. **OEM / redistribution** — per end-customer, product line, or revenue band
4. **Subscription** — annual right to use + updates and support tier

## Support and trademarks

Commercial agreements may optionally include:

- Priority support and security advisories
- Certified releases and migration guidance
- Trademark license for “Picard” branding in co-marketing (separate policy)

Trademark use without permission is not granted by AGPL or this template.

## Contributing

Contributions to the public repository are accepted under the understanding that
they are licensed under **AGPL-3.0** unless a separate Contributor License
Agreement (CLA) is in place. If you adopt dual licensing, add `CONTRIBUTING.md`
with inbound rights language before soliciting external patches.

## Versioning

Commercial licenses are usually tied to:

- A **major.minor** release line, or
- A **subscription** period with upgrades as defined in the order form

AGPL obligations for code you received under AGPL are **not** removed retroactively;
the commercial license applies to uses that would otherwise require a separate grant.
